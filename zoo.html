Feeding and Petting Areas

About Our Feeding and Petting Areas

At WonderOasis Zoo, we provide interactive feeding and petting areas where visitors can get up close and personal with some of our friendly animals. These areas are designed to create memorable experiences for visitors of all ages while ensuring the safety and well-being of our animals.

Age-Appropriate Activities:
  • Children (Ages 3-10): Gentle petting and feeding activities with small animals like rabbits, guinea pigs, and goats.
  • Teens and Adults: Feeding larger animals like Deer and Giraffe under staff supervision.
Rules and Guidelines:
  • Always follow the instructions of our staff members.
  • Wash your hands before and after interacting with the animals.
  • Do not feed animals anything other than the food provided by the zoo.
  • Be gentle and respectful towards the animals at all times.
  • Children under 12 must be accompanied by an adult.

Jungle Ranger

Little explorers, big jungle adventures! Step into a lush tropical world where young rangers take the lead. With mini jeeps navigating gentle trails, lifelike animal replicas, and immersive jungle sounds, this ride offers a thrilling yet safe experience. Perfect for curious kids, it creates the feeling of a real jungle adventure without live animals—allowing them to explore and learn at their own pace. Adventure awaits around every leafy corner!

This attraction is suitable for ages 6 and up.
Children under 1.2m must be accompanied by an adult.

Educational Programs

At WonderOasis Zoo, we are committed to educating visitors about wildlife conservation and animal care through engaging programs and activities.

Workshops and Activities

  • Animal Care Workshops: Learn how zookeepers care for animals, including feeding and habitat maintenance.
  • Conservation Talks: Discover the importance of protecting endangered species through expert-led discussions.
  • Interactive Learning: Participate in activities like building birdhouses or creating enrichment toys for animals.

School Programs

  • Field Trips: Guided tours and interactive sessions tailored for students of all ages.
  • Zoo Keeper for a Day: Experience the daily responsibilities of a zookeeper.
  • Educational Kits: Take-home kits with fun activities and quizzes about animals and conservation.

Special Events

  • Wildlife Awareness Days: Events dedicated to specific animals or conservation themes.
  • Animal Feeding Demonstrations: Watch zookeepers feed animals and learn about their diets.
  • Meet the Experts: Interact with zoologists, veterinarians, and conservationists.

Community Involvement

  • Volunteer Programs: Contribute to zoo operations and conservation projects.
  • Adopt an Animal Program: Symbolically adopt an animal to support its care and conservation.
